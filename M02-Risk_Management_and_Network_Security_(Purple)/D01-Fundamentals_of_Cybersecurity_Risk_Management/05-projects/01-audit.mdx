# ISO 27001 Security Audit for IBC

## Context

As part of the ongoing efforts to enhance security, IBC has engaged your consulting firm to conduct a detailed ISO 27001 audit on its information security management system (ISMS). This audit is aimed at ensuring that the company's policies, procedures, and security controls are in compliance with the requirements of ISO 27001. The audit will help identify any gaps in the current system, assess the overall effectiveness of existing controls, and recommend necessary actions to address non-conformities.

IBC is a financial institution that processes sensitive customer data and transactions, making the security of their information systems critical for both regulatory compliance and business continuity.

## Consultant's Notes

- **Target**: Information Security Management System (ISMS).
- **Scope**:
  - **Review of information security policies and procedures**.
  - **Evaluation of risk management practices**.
  - **Verification of security incident management**.
  - **Analysis of access control and user management processes**.
- **Key Focus Areas**:
  - Confidentiality, integrity, and availability of customer and business data.
  - Compliance with legal and regulatory requirements.
  - Incident response and vulnerability management.
- **ISO 27001 Context**:
  - The audit will focus on the key controls specified by ISO 27001, particularly those related to risk management, access controls, and incident response.
  - The audit will include a Test of Design (TOD) to verify that policies and processes are in place and a Test of Effectiveness (TOE) to ensure that these controls are functioning as intended.

## Task

You are currently conducting an ISO 27001 audit and will focus on the following areas:

1. **Review of information security policies and procedures**: You will evaluate the existing security policies to determine if they are comprehensive, up-to-date, and in line with ISO 27001 requirements.

2. **Evaluation of risk management practices**: You will review the risk assessment processes to ensure that risks are properly identified, assessed, and managed, and that the appropriate mitigation strategies are in place.

3. **Verification of security incident management**: You will examine how security incidents are reported, managed, and resolved. This includes assessing the documentation of incidents and whether lessons learned are integrated into future security practices.

4. **Analysis of access control and user management processes**: You will verify the effectiveness of access controls, including how user privileges are assigned, managed, and revoked, as well as how access to sensitive systems and data is monitored.
